**Title:** GMI 481E Marine Insurance

**Course Name:** Marine Insurance  
**Course Code:** GMI 481E  
**Semester:** 7-8  
**Local Credits:** 2  
**ECTS Credits:** 4  
**Course Language:** English  
**Course Type:** Elective  
**Course Prerequisites:** None  

**Course Description:**  
The course covers the concept of insurance, insurance law, definitions, and terminology. It includes Marine Insurance Law, Hull Insurance, Marine Cargo Insurance, Protection and Indemnity (P&I), and the relationship between technical ship management rules and insurance rules. It also addresses documentation for evidence, conflicts in Marine Insurance, and analysis of court decisions.

**Course Objectives:**  
1. To teach the types and scopes of marine insurances and their application in ship management.  
2. To help students understand the relationship between insurance rules and ship management.

**Course Learning Outcomes:**  
Students who pass the course will be able to:  
I. Understand insurance terminology and the responsibilities of the parties.  
II. Comprehend maritime risks covered by insurances.  
III. Learn about different types of maritime insurance.  
IV. Understand the relationship between insurance rules and technical ship management rules, and learn how to document insurance losses.

**Course Plan:**  
1. Insurance concept, insurance law, definitions, legal sources of insurance  
2. Insurance terminology, abbreviations  
3. Marine Insurance Law; definitions, terminology, history, legal sources  
4. Marine Insurance Law; types, contracts, principles  
5. Hull Insurance; types, hull underwriting  
6. Hull Insurance; institute hull clauses  
7. Marine Cargo Insurance; incoterms, exclusions  
8. Marine Cargo Insurance; institute cargo clauses  
9. Protection and Indemnity (P&I); P&I Clubs, history, system, coverage  
10. P&I Clubs; typical rules, claims  
11. FDND (Freight, Demurrage and Defense Insurance) and other insurances  
12. Relationship between insurance rules and ship management  
13. Documentation for objective evidence  
14. Marine Insurance conflicts and court decisions
