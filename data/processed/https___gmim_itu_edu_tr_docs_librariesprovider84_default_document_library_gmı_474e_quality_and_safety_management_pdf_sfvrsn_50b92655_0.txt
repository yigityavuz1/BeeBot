**Course Name:** Quality and Safety Management Systems  
**Course Code:** GMI 474E  
**Semester:** 7-8  
**Local Credits:** 2  
**ECTS Credits:** 4  
**Course Language:** English  
**Course Type:** Elective  
**Course Prerequisites:** None  

**Course Description:**  
- Management systems  
- Maritime stakeholders  
- Ship safety management system  
- International standards: ISO 9001, ISO 14001, OHSAS 18001, ISO 50001  
- Integrated management systems  
- Key performance indicators  
- Process improvement techniques  
- Corrective and preventive actions  
- Emergency management process  
- Ship maintenance planning process  
- Training and safety awareness  
- Management system inspections  

**Course Objectives:**  
1. Teach the ship safety management system  
2. Teach process improvement via international standards  
3. Teach the requirements of company/ship audit  

**Course Learning Outcomes:**  
Students will be able to:  
I. Understand main principles of management systems  
II. Implement ship safety management systems  
III. Meet requirements of international standards  
IV. Implement integrated management systems  
V. Conduct ship management system audits  

**Course Plan:**  
1. Management systems

**Relationship to Marine Engineering Student Outcomes:**  
1. Identify, formulate, and solve complex engineering problems  
2. Apply engineering design considering public health, safety, and welfare  
3. Communicate effectively with a range of audiences  
4. Recognize ethical and professional responsibilities in engineering  
5. Function effectively on a team  
6. Develop and conduct appropriate experimentation and analyze data  
7. Acquire and apply new knowledge as needed  
8. Operate and maintain marine engines and systems effectively and safely
