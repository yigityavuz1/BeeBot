**Course Description:**  
The scope of organic chemistry includes chemical bonding, atomic properties of carbon, hybrid orbitals, aliphatic hydrocarbons (alkanes, cycloalkanes), alkenes, alkynes, halogenated compounds, alcohols, diols, ethers, aromatic compounds, aldehydes, ketones, carboxylic acids and derivatives, amines, and polymers.

**Course Objectives:**  
1. To teach the basic concepts and principles of organic chemistry.  
2. To provide theoretical and practical knowledge together.  
3. To improve the ability of organic problem-solving and critical decision-making.  
4. To explain the importance of organic chemistry in daily life.  
5. To encourage logical and positive thinking regarding the importance of organic chemistry in engineering.

**Course Learning Outcomes:**  
Students who pass the course satisfactorily can:  
1. Learn and apply the basic concepts and principles of organic chemistry.  
2. Integrate and interpret theoretical and practical knowledge.  
3. Develop problem-solving skills in organic chemistry.  
4. Understand the relevance of organic chemistry in everyday life.  
5. Think logically and positively about organic chemistry's significance in engineering.  
6. Apply theoretical organic knowledge in laboratory and industrial settings.  
7. Scale laboratory reactions to industrial production.

**Course Plan:**

- Week 7: Aromatic Compounds  
- Week 8: Aromatic Compounds
